How It Works

Ants explore both cliffs, reinforce pheromone trails toward the gap, and link together when they reach the attachment zone. Each new body extends the living chain until it can lock onto the far side, allowing the colony to cross.

  • Foragers wander the ledges and call others with pheromones.
  • Attachment-ready ants latch onto the last segment of the bridge.
  • The bridge sags under its own weight until the span is complete.
  • Once stable, traffic begins streaming over the living structure.

Inspired by real army-ant bridge formation. Built with canvas rendering and simple swarm heuristics.
