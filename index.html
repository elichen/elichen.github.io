Yen vs Dollar: Currency Dynamics

The Japanese Yen has generally been weaker than the US Dollar in recent years. This analysis explores the key factors behind this trend and provides historical context for understanding currency dynamics between these two major economies.

JPY per 1 USD (2014-2024)

Key Factors Influencing Yen Weakness

Monetary Policy Divergence

Bank of Japan: Ultra-low rates
US Federal Reserve: Rate hikes

Economic Growth

US: Stronger growth
Japan: Sluggish growth

Inflation Rates

Japan: Deflation/low inflation
US: Higher inflation

Trade Balance

Japan: Increased energy imports
US: Improving trade balance

Safe-Haven Status

Dollar: Primary reserve currency
Yen: Secondary safe-haven

Historical Context: Yen-Dollar Exchange Rate Timeline

1971

End of Bretton Woods system. Yen begins to float against the Dollar.

1985

Plaza Accord leads to Yen appreciation against the Dollar.

1995

Yen reaches post-war high of 79.75 per Dollar.

2002

Japan's "Lost Decade" continues, keeping Yen weak.

2011

Fukushima disaster leads to Yen appreciation due to repatriation flows.

2012

Shinzo Abe elected, introduces "Abenomics" leading to Yen depreciation.

2020-2024

COVID-19 pandemic and recovery. Diverging monetary policies lead to further Yen weakness.

Conclusion

The Yen's weakness against the Dollar stems from a complex interplay of economic factors and policy decisions. While short-term fluctuations occur, the long-term trend reflects diverging monetary policies, economic growth patterns, and global events. This analysis provides insights into the Yen-Dollar dynamic and broader principles of currency valuation in international economics.
