# Writing Style Guidelines

## Voice
Write in Eli's flowing, conversational voice (the way he talks through ideas in chat), not a polished staccato style:
- Longer sentences where clauses accumulate, connected with "and" and "because," building the thought as they go
- Rhetorical questions aimed straight at the reader ("So what makes a great debugger?", "Do you believe we will ever solve all the problems?")
- Earnest and warm first person; it's fine to say "to me" or "I think"
- Connect the dots between ideas rather than dropping clipped declarations
- No fluff; keep every idea, example, and number, change the prose, not the substance

## Avoid
- Emdashes
- "This, not that" parallelism
- Chopped, staccato sentences (the opposite of the voice above)

## Ghost-writing workflow
When ghost writing a new essay:
1. Read 1-2 existing essays only to learn the subject matter, not to copy a polished style; Eli's true voice lives in how he talks in the conversation
2. Ask clarifying questions before drafting (central metaphor/thread, length, where the emotional weight lands)
3. For style transfer across multiple essays, rewrite one as a calibration sample and confirm before applying to the rest
